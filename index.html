Korean Chinese, Chinese Korean

What does it mean to belong to two countries when neither citizenship nor ancestry provides a final answer?

Born in Yanbian, China, into a Korean-speaking family, Yongjun Zhu was educated in Korean, studied in Korea and America, and later returned to Korea as a professor. Moving through childhood, language, migration, family ancestry, and the keeping of his name, this is one person's long inquiry into identity — what we inherit, what others call us, and what we choose to carry.
